Who We Are

More than a club —
a community.

Nursing programs are intense. Between clinical placements, academic demands, and the emotional weight of caring for others, it's easy to feel overwhelmed — or alone. Safe Space Nurses was created by nursing students, for nursing students.

Every SSN event is grounded in emotion-focused coping theory and evidence-based approaches to mental health. This site is a resource hub for our community and any nursing student seeking support.

📋 Ongoing Research Study — Pending Ethics Approval
Safe Space Nurses is conducting a pilot mixed-methods study assessing how peer-led events affect perceived stress, sense of belonging, and early burnout markers in undergraduate nursing students at York University.
🚶‍♀️
Wellness Walks & Stretch Breaks
Movement-based resets between demanding clinical and academic blocks — shown to reduce cortisol and restore focus.
💬
Peer Reflection Sessions
Facilitated spaces to process clinical experiences with peers who understand the emotional demands of nursing training.
🤝
Community Building Events
Free, low-pressure social events designed to reduce isolation and strengthen your sense of belonging in the nursing community.
🔬
Evidence-Based Design
All SSN programming is informed by validated research on nursing student mental health and emotion-focused coping theory.

    Stress & Burnout Self-Care

    Evidence-informed strategies built around the specific stressors of nursing training — small, sustainable practices.

    • Schedule rest the same way you schedule class — as a non-negotiable block
    • After a hard clinical shift, debrief with a peer before going home alone
    • 5-min journaling: what happened today + one thing you did well
    • Notice early burnout signs: detachment, cynicism, reduced empathy, exhaustion
    • Use the 5-4-3-2-1 grounding tool — see below ↓

    Grounding Tool

    5-4-3-2-1 Grounding

    This evidence-based technique anchors you to the present moment when anxiety or overwhelm spikes. Move through each step slowly.

    5
    👀 Things you can See
    Look around and slowly name 5 things you can see right now. Notice colour, shape, texture, light. Take your time with each one.
    No rush — breathe slowly as you go
